Agentic Delivery Architecture

The unit of delivery is changing. The future shape is discovered, not forecast.

Traditional delivery split work across a stack of specialists. Agile brought those specialists into a two-pizza team. AI now changes the unit of engineering work again, the mix of skills that must sit together, and the boundaries between roles. What it does not hand us is a new headcount ratio we can design from in advance.

So we sell squads a specific way. We do not move every client to a two-person team. We find the smallest safe and effective shape for each workflow, record why we chose it as a decision, and support it with an engineered system. This page is about engineering delivery, not a forecast of operational or support headcount.

Do not predict the future organisation from today's job titles. Discover it from the work.

Emerging delivery shapes

There is no fixed squad size. Depending on the work, the right shape might be a full multidisciplinary squad, a smaller AI-amplified squad, a domain expert and an engineer, a single accountable professional orchestrating agents, or a deliberately larger team where judgement, customer care or operational risk require it.

{% include diagrams/squad-evolution.html %}

Team composition becomes dynamic and work-specific. The smallest shapes are possible, not mandatory, and for most production services they are some way off. Our job is to find the smallest safe and effective shape for each workflow, not to march everyone down to two people.

What is knowable, and what is not

Honest advisory separates the two. We will not sell you a workforce forecast we cannot stand behind.

Knowable now
  • Repetitive coordination and production work will be increasingly automated
  • Role boundaries will blur
  • Workflow knowledge becomes more valuable
  • Architecture, controls, data and support must become reusable
  • People will supervise, evaluate and improve agent-supported work
  • Institutional knowledge and future experts must be grown on purpose
Not knowable now
  • The aggregate number of roles removed or created
  • Which functions contract most
  • The stable ratio of humans to agents
  • The future canonical squad size
  • The exact skills mix in three to five years
  • Whether productivity becomes growth, better service or cost out

This is why we treat the position that the change is transformational but its medium-term structure remains unclear as more credible than confident workforce forecasts. A figure like tens of thousands of people needing AI skills is directionally right. It does not tell you which skills, at what depth, or for which redesigned work. That is what the discovery finds.

The smaller the cell, the more the organisation must carry

A small cell is not a smaller version of a squad. It is a squad that has moved most of its context, constraints and controls out of people's heads and into the organisation around it. Any lean shape works only when five things can be supplied on demand, not rediscovered per project.

Trusted context

The cell inherits the reasoning behind the system it is changing, not just the code. Every material decision is recorded as a Transformation Decision Record: executable decision context an agent and a human can both act on. See TDRs and the ledger →

Reusable knowledge

It does not start from a blank page. Knowledge Intelligence and the decision graph connect decisions, capabilities, evidence and outcomes, so domain knowledge is drawn on rather than reconstructed each time a cell forms.

Constraints

Autonomy needs boundaries that travel with the work. Each agent runs under a machine-readable Agent Contract: mandate, permissions, operating boundary and revocation, with the actor verified by Know Your Agent before it acts.

Evidence

Decisions, outcomes and model versions are written to the shared trust ledger, so what the cell did, and under what authority, is provable after the fact rather than argued in a meeting.

Escalation

When a decision is novel or falls outside the boundary, the cell does not improvise. Architecture, risk, legal and compliance are on call through the capability mesh, and exceptions route to an accountable human.

Take any one away

and the cell stops being accountable autonomy and becomes unmanaged risk. This is why the model is a system design question first, and a staffing question second.

One shared system. Many possible shapes.

The platform does not assume a team size. It supplies context, constraints, evidence, reusable knowledge and escalation to whatever shape the work needs, from a full team to a single accountable professional. The cell sits inside a paved road, wrapped by an accountability backbone.

{% include diagrams/agentic-cell-system.html %}

1. Agentic paved road

A pre-integrated route from idea to production: approved models, tools and connectors; reusable agent skills and Domain Building Blocks; isolated environments; automated testing, evaluation and policy checks; deployment, observability and cost metering; versioned prompts, models, data, code and agent configurations. Without it, every cell rebuilds a fragile local stack.

2. Trust and control plane

Controls travel with the work, not through meetings: agent identity and Know Your Agent; machine-readable Agent Contracts; mandates, permissions and operating boundaries; TDRs for material decisions; evidence, outcomes and model versions written to the trust ledger; revocation, rollback and kill switches.

3. Production support

A lean cell cannot recreate a bank's operational machinery. It draws on shared service ownership and on-call; incident, problem and change pathways designed for agents; resilience and recovery testing; agent failure and degradation patterns; human takeover and exception handling; common operational telemetry.

4. Enterprise capability mesh

Architecture, data, cyber, risk, legal, compliance and procurement move from permanent seats and committee gates into reusable services: controls as code; architecture fitness functions; approved data products; reusable contractual and regulatory patterns; automated assurance; specialists on call for exceptions and novel decisions.

5. Workforce and learning pipeline

A senior model must create the next generation, not consume expertise: rotating apprentices and emerging engineers; recorded decisions and reusable agent skills; structured shadowing of domain experts; progression from operator to orchestrator to accountable owner; deliberate transfer of supplier knowledge into the client.

The centre gets smaller, and more capable

It owns the paved road, the reference architecture and the trust ledger. It does not own the squad's backlog.

The work changes shape, not just size

When agents produce most of the code, the scarce human work moves up. Wherever a cell is lean, its people are not doing the old jobs faster. They are doing different work.

From producing to framing

The Product Owner owns the problem and the outcome. The Forward Deployed Engineer frames the work for agents, sets the constraints and checks the evidence. Neither is measured by lines produced.

Judgement and systems thinking

The value sits in the trade-offs an agent cannot own: what good looks like, where the risk really is, how one decision moves the wider system. This is why lean shapes are senior by default.

Governing autonomous execution

The human role becomes directing and assuring agents that act: setting mandates, reading outcomes, intervening when the evidence says so. Governing execution, not performing it.

This is a talent redesign, not a headcount cut. If the first rungs of the engineering ladder were where people learned the craft, and agents now do that work, the ladder has to be rebuilt rather than removed. Emerging engineers learn by orchestrating and reviewing agents, shadowing domain experts and reading the recorded decisions, progressing from operator to orchestrator to accountable owner. Leadership shifts with them: managing mandates and outcomes, not seats and utilisation. The operating model sets out how that apprenticeship is rebuilt.

We discover the shape, we do not prescribe it

The advisory question is not how to move you to a two-person squad. It is how to find the smallest safe and effective human configuration for each workflow, while building a platform that supports several operating shapes. Each pass is recorded as a decision, so the shape is evidence-led, not asserted.
