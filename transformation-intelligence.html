Transformation Intelligence

Your organisation has no shared record of why it believes what it believes.

Strategy becomes programmes, architecture, controls, customer journeys and increasingly machine action. But the judgement connecting them is scattered across decks, meetings and people. Organisations do not fail because they stop making decisions. They fail because they lose their shared understanding as those decisions accumulate.

A Shared Trust Ledger is the connected record of what was decided, why, by whom, against which evidence, and whether it remained true when executed. Transformation Intelligence is the discipline that creates and governs it.

Every organisation lives in this loop

Something changes, you gather evidence, you form a shared view, you decide, you act, you learn, and the world changes again. The challenge is not making decisions. It is keeping a shared understanding as the world changes and those decisions pile up.

Every decision is an entry in the ledger. MTDR is how you record it.

Every significant decision contributes another entry to the Shared Trust Ledger. MTDR is the open standard for recording those entries: a Transformation Decision Record captures enough judgement to defend, execute, monitor, challenge and later supersede a decision, without forcing a new platform or operating-model programme. It is MIT-licensed and tool-neutral, and works alongside your ADRs, Confluence and GRC platform.

What one entry holds

  1. Decision and status
  2. Problem and value thesis
  3. Options considered and foreclosed
  4. Decision owner and authority
  5. Obligations, risks and affected outcomes
  6. Evidence available at the time
  7. Decision and accepted consequences
  8. Controls and enforcement locus
  9. Signals that confirm or falsify it
  10. Review, reversal and supersession

A runtime log shows what happened. A decision record shows why the organisation believed it was legitimate to let it happen.
