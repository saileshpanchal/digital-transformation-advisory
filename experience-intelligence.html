Experience Intelligence

Understanding the outcomes people need and the journeys they actually live, so propositions include those most easily excluded and earn understanding, agency, and trust.

{% include cta-calendly.html kind="strategy_call" loc="exp-int-hero" label="Discuss a challenge" %} See all Intelligences

Does this work for the people it is easiest to exclude?

That is the question this lens keeps in front of a bank. A proposition that works for someone without a fixed address, conventional documents, or a smartphone exposes the assumptions an ordinary happy path hides. Experience Intelligence reads a bank through the outcome a person actually needs, not the product a bank happens to sell.

What this lens governs

Good outcomes are designed into a proposition, not evidenced after the harm is done.

Desired outcomes

Define the intended outcome, the people it serves, the risks, and the support model before choosing a platform or building a journey.

Lived journeys

Follow the end-to-end experience a person actually lives, across assisted web, telephone, and branch, not only the device-first path.

Inclusion and agency

Design from the excluded edge, preserve human agency when agents act, and keep a clear route to a person.

Evidence of outcome

Generate evidence while decisions are made, so a bank can show the outcome happened, not just that a box was ticked.
