Leave no customer behind

Experience Intelligence

The question: will the new experience deliver demonstrably better outcomes, for everyone it touches?

Every transformation changes somebody's experience: customers, colleagues, partners, vulnerable consumers, regulators. Experience Intelligence designs, tests and proves those experiences before implementation, so innovation delivers demonstrably good outcomes, not unintended harm. It is not user-centred design; it is outcome-centred transformation — the board's answer to Consumer Duty in the age of AI.

Three questions a board should ask of any change

Consumer Duty asks whether consumers got a good outcome, not whether they liked the screen.

Does it deliver a good outcome?

The outcome the journey should produce, defined and tested up front, not audited after launch.

For everyone, or only the easy customers?

Who is excluded, underserved or left behind, and whether the journey catches them.

Can we prove it before we build?

Outcomes evidenced on a working prototype, not asserted in a deck or discovered in production.

How we work

Evidence-led, not workshop-led.

Understand

Observe real behaviour, from operational and journey data, not opinions.

Design

Create alternative experiences, including the ones for people the model leaves out.

Prove

Test them with data and executable prototypes before anything is built.

Measure

Evidence consumer outcomes continuously, not once at launch.

Prove the experience before you build it

We don't debate experiences in workshops. We prototype, measure and prove them, so the board decides on evidence.

Hypothesis→ Prototype→ Measure→ Evidence→ Decision

Every significant customer or colleague journey, from onboarding and complaint handling to vulnerability, fraud recovery and SME lending, can be designed, prototyped, simulated on synthetic data, measured and refined in the Accountable Bank before a pound of production investment. Not Figma screens: executable journeys with evidence the board can read.

Consumer Duty, evidenced — not asserted

Consumer Duty isn't about avoiding harm. It's about creating good outcomes, measuring them, proving them and improving them. Can consumers understand? We measure it. Can vulnerable customers succeed? We observe it. Does the experience improve outcomes? We test it. Can we prove it? It's evidenced.

Illustrative. People grasped the benefit but misread the fee and what the agent did. That is the evidence to design out before launch, not after a complaint.

Who is missing?

Most programmes optimise for the customers who already use the service. The more strategic question is who doesn't — and who you could now serve because AI has changed the cost of serving them.

Illustrative. Existing customers, colleagues, the vulnerable, the digitally excluded, the underserved, and customers no one could serve before. Experience Coverage measures all of it: coverage, accessibility, understanding, trust and outcomes, not just usability.

Inclusion isn't only the right thing; it is new revenue. Customers who once failed onboarding, segments that were uneconomic to serve manually, vulnerable customers needing assisted journeys, older customers supported through hybrid human-and-AI journeys: AI changes the economics, so Experience Intelligence feeds Value and Financial Intelligence, not just compliance.

Begin by proving the experience

Transformation shouldn't begin with technology. It should begin by proving that the future experience delivers demonstrably better outcomes for consumers, colleagues and the institution.

Experience designs it. Platform delivers it. Capability enables it. Control governs it. Financial funds it. And Trust is what a good experience earns. Leave no customer behind.
