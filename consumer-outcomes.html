Banking should work for the people it is easiest to exclude.

Good consumer outcomes are designed into a proposition, not evidenced after the harm is done. We help banks and fintechs design, test, and evidence outcomes around real consumer needs before delivery choices harden into code, process, and cost.

Consumer outcomes begin at proposition design

The FCA's Consumer Duty expects firms to design for consumer needs across the journey, check that communications are understood, and evidence those judgements with testing rather than assertion. That is a design input, not a post-launch audit. It belongs to Experience and Trust Intelligence, and we treat it as a position, not a compliance task.

What we ask banks to do

Six principles we advocate, before a proposition becomes code, process, and cost.

Outcomes before implementation

Define the intended outcome, the risks, the support model, and the measures before choosing a platform or building a journey.

Design from the excluded edge

A proposition that works for someone without a fixed address, conventional documents, or a smartphone exposes the assumptions a happy path hides.

Human agency when agents act

Consumers should understand when an agent acts, what authority it has, how to intervene, and where to find a person.

Evidence as responsibility

Evidence by construction helps a bank learn and stay accountable. It is not a shortcut to compliance or a claim of regulatory approval.

Lived experience over synthetic confidence

Test important propositions with relevant consumers and specialist organisations before recommending delivery.

Inclusion with economic discipline

Better access should reduce avoidable exceptions and rework, not transfer hidden cost to charities or consumers.

A Place to Start Blueprint

One person, one outcome, one challenge to current banking assumptions.

Help one person without a fixed address, conventional documents, or a smartphone gain an ordinary basic bank account, through assisted web, telephone, and branch. A supporter can submit bounded evidence but cannot reach the account. The bank colleague makes the accountable customer-due-diligence decision. The applicant keeps control, and the bank keeps accountability.

It leads with the human outcome, then the bank outcome: lower and more consistent exception-handling effort, shown with illustrative assumptions. We label the journey Blueprint until it works end to end. See it among the journeys.

What changes when agents act

A focus group can tell you whether someone understood a screen. It cannot tell you whether they understood what an agent did for them, and could step in.

Comprehension

Did the consumer understand what the agent did on their behalf, and why?

Consent and control

Did they agree, and was there a gate before anything material happened?

Intervention and support

Could a consumer, including a vulnerable one, step in, change course, and reach a person?

How we help: advisory, then proof

We advise on the position, then test the proposition on the same working platform that runs our demonstrations, before the build hardens. Five short stages on one proposition.

1. Proposition setup

Purpose, target market, exclusions, vulnerability hypotheses, pricing assumptions, and the outcomes it should produce.

2. Journey walkthrough

Onboarding, eligibility, disclosures, the key choices, the agent's actions and approval gates, support, and exit.

3. Variant testing

Compare versions of a message, a control, a fee explanation, or how an agent explains what it is about to do.

4. Session capture

Record the confusion points, the drop-offs, the questions people ask, and how confident they felt.

5. Outcome evidence pack

A board-ready view of risks, observed harms, mitigations, and the design changes they drove, plus a vendor-neutral working specification.

The signature output is a misunderstanding heatmap across the journey: where people grasp the headline benefit but misread the fee, the cooling-off terms, the support steps, or what the agent just did. Read how we test, learn, and evidence.
