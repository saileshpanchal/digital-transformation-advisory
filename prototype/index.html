How we govern decisions

Decisions are recorded at planning, before execution. After execution we verify against every decision to catch drift. Prior decisions are reused in future planning. We run DTA itself this way.

Record at planning› Agree the decisions› Execute› Verify, catch drift› Reuse in planning

From decision graph to operational graph

Decisions are recorded as Transformation Decision Records under the open MTDR standard, then joined in one governed graph to the policies they cite, the regulatory obligations those policies satisfy, the controls and evidence, the risks each decision carries, and the services each decision governs. When a service fails, the blast radius and the obligations at risk are a query, not an investigation. Demonstrated in the Accountable Bank over a curated dependency catalogue and synthetic data; self-registering services and live telemetry are target-state.
