# DTA Controlled-Evolution Website Design

**Status:** Proposed for review

**Date:** 19 June 2026

**Scope:** DTA corporate website and its relationship to the Clean-Room Demonstration

## Decision

DTA will lead with strategic advisory. Staffing and agentic squads will remain visible, independently purchasable services, but they will support the advisory position rather than define the firm.

The website will evolve rather than rebrand. It will retain the established ideas of Owned Autonomy, C3, the Clean-Room Demonstration, Proposition Blueprints, and the Glass Tube. It will reorganise those ideas around a simpler market promise:

> DTA helps banks and fintechs define the outcome, prove the hard parts, and build the capability to deliver it.

The working platform proves DTA's advice. DTA does not present itself as a software platform or data-mesh vendor.

## Objectives

The update must:

1. Establish DTA as a senior strategic advisory firm within the first screen.
2. Show that DTA can turn advice into working, inspectable proof.
3. Keep staffing, specialist talent, and managed squads easy to find and buy.
4. Make the Clean-Room Demonstration a source of credibility, not the corporate category.
5. Use *A Place to Start* as the flagship example of human outcomes, operating economics, and reusable architecture.
6. Preserve the website's claim discipline: demonstrated, illustrative, and blueprint capabilities remain visibly distinct.

## Positioning

### Corporate category

**Independent strategy and executable architecture for agentic banking.**

### Core promise

> We help banks and fintechs design differentiated propositions, prove the difficult controls before committing to build, and create the architecture, operating model, and capability to scale them.

### Governing idea

**Owned Autonomy** remains DTA's doctrine: agentic capability a bank owns rather than rents, accountable and sovereign.

### Proof method

**Evidence by construction** describes how DTA proves decisions and controls. It is a method and differentiator, not a promise that a regulator has accepted or certified an artefact.

### Flagship proof point

**A Place to Start** shows the position in human terms: help one person without a fixed address, conventional documents, or a smartphone gain access to an ordinary basic bank account, while preserving the bank's accountability and the applicant's control.

## Audiences and buying moments

### Strategic advisory buyers

- Bank and fintech CEOs and business-unit leaders shaping growth or transformation.
- CTOs and CIOs choosing architecture, platforms, and migration paths.
- COOs and transformation leaders reducing operating friction and exception cost.
- CDOs and CISOs governing data, authority, evidence, and agent action.
- Product, Consumer Duty, and financial-inclusion leaders designing new propositions.

Their buying moments include a new proposition, an agentic strategy, a platform decision, a failing transformation, a regulatory concern, or a need to reduce the cost of manual exceptions.

### Staffing and squad buyers

- Technology and transformation leaders who need senior specialists quickly.
- Product and engineering leaders who need a managed squad for a bounded outcome.
- Executives who need a fractional technology, architecture, payments, or transformation leader.

Staffing can begin as a standalone engagement. It should also provide a route from advice into delivery without implying that clients must buy a transformation programme.

## Commercial architecture

The website will present three routes in this order:

### 1. Strategic advisory

The primary route. DTA helps clients define the proposition, economics, target architecture, operating model, decision rights, controls, and transformation sequence.

Example engagements:

- Agentic banking and AI strategy.
- Proposition and business-model design.
- Independent target architecture and platform decisions.
- Payments, economic-crime, and data strategy.
- Operating-model and transformation advisory.
- Consumer outcomes and financial inclusion.

### 2. Proposition proof

DTA turns the strategy into an inspectable decision artefact through a Proposition Blueprint, Clean-Room Demonstration, customer-outcome testing, or Glass Tube pilot.

The client receives evidence, design decisions, and a vendor-neutral working specification. Public wording must distinguish a demonstrated capability from a blueprint.

### 3. Talent and squads

DTA supplies senior individuals, fractional leaders, forward platform engineers, or managed agentic squads. This route supports delivery and remains independently purchasable.

The copy must avoid body-shop language. It should sell accountable capability and bounded outcomes, not CV volume or cheap capacity.

## Information architecture

The primary navigation should become:

- **Home**
- **Advisory**
- **Propositions**
- **Talent & Squads**
- **Insights**
- **Book a strategy call**

The Advisory page or menu will route visitors to:

- Strategic and transformation advisory.
- Approach and operating model.
- Customer outcomes.
- Capability Intelligence.

The Propositions route will retain `/composable-bank/` and its existing subpages. Platform, Data Products, and Resilience remain supporting proof inside the demonstration rather than top-level corporate offers.

The website should introduce a dedicated Talent & Squads page. This page will distinguish embedded specialists, fractional leadership, and managed squads without displacing strategic advisory from the homepage.

## Homepage design

### 1. Hero

**Proposed headline**

> Design the outcome. Prove the hard parts. Build the bank that can deliver it.

**Proposed supporting copy**

> DTA helps banks and fintechs turn agentic ideas into governed propositions, working evidence, and the organisational capability to scale them, without surrendering control of the technology beneath them.

**Calls to action**

- Primary: **Discuss a strategic challenge**
- Secondary: **Explore the working propositions**
- Tertiary text link: **Need talent or a squad?**

The hero must not lead with staffing, architecture jargon, or the platform.

### 2. The problem DTA solves

Explain the board-level tension in plain language:

- Agents are moving from advice to action.
- Banks need commercial value without losing accountability or control.
- Platform commitments arrive before teams have proved the proposition, controls, and operating consequences.

Close with the Accountability-Sovereignty Principle: **You cannot be accountable for what you do not control.**

### 3. Three ways DTA helps

Present three cards in the commercial order:

1. **Advise**: define the proposition, target architecture, operating model, and transformation path.
2. **Prove**: test the journey, controls, economics, and customer outcomes before implementation hardens.
3. **Deliver**: provide the leaders, specialists, and managed squads that turn the decision into working capability.

Each card should stand alone. Cross-links should show how the routes combine.

### 4. Flagship story: A Place to Start

Introduce one synthetic person experiencing homelessness. Lead with the human outcome, then show the bank outcome:

- An assisted route that does not assume a fixed address, standard documents, or a personal smartphone.
- Bank-owned customer-due-diligence decisions.
- A supporter who can submit bounded evidence but cannot access the account.
- An ordinary basic bank account and a safe servicing route.
- Lower and more consistent exception-handling effort, shown with illustrative assumptions.

The section will link to the proposition page. Until the complete journey works, both locations must label it **Blueprint**.

### 5. Strategy that can be inspected

Reframe the current showcase band around the advisory advantage:

> We do not ask a bank to accept an architecture on a slide. We use a working proving ground to test the propositions, authority boundaries, data products, customer outcomes, and migration decisions before commitment.

Retain the Clean-Room boundary: synthetic data, test consumers, production-grade control design, and no production banking service.

### 6. Transformation and staffing

Show how a client can continue after proof:

- DTA can advise the transformation and remain independent of implementation vendors.
- DTA can supply fractional leaders or specialists.
- DTA can form a managed squad around a bounded outcome.
- The client retains the working capability.

### 7. Leadership and insight

Retain the founder track record and current insight feed. Connect the biography to the market promise: DTA combines bank leadership, payments, economic crime, architecture, technology, and transformation experience.

## Propositions experience

### Landing-page message

> Propositions first. Architecture proven underneath.

Supporting copy should explain that visitors can explore how a proposition creates value, operates under control, and informs a vendor-neutral working specification before a bank commits to build.

### Proposition organisation

Describe each proposition by the business outcome it tests:

- **A Place to Start**: inclusion and lower exception-handling cost.
- **Family Wealth**: relationship depth and deposit retention.
- **Cross-Border Real-Time Payments**: payment experience and deposit acquisition.
- **Premium Yacht**: delegated authority without loss of customer control.
- **International Student**: identity, funding, and cross-border onboarding.
- **Franchise Network**: network-wide operations, labelled Blueprint.

Status badges remain mandatory. The page must never use *live* to mean demonstrated.

### Evidence by construction

Add a section that shows the safe shape of an evidence trace:

- Authenticated actor.
- Bounded authority.
- Evaluated policy.
- Decision and reason.
- Evidence retained.
- Sensitive information excluded from the public projection.

Before safe evidence download exists, the page may display an illustrative, labelled trace. It must not promise a signed package, regulatory acceptance, certification, or a download generated by every journey.

## A Place to Start proposition page

The page will follow one person through this sequence:

1. The person and the practical barriers they face.
2. Assisted web, telephone, and branch options.
3. Consent and minimal evidence collection.
4. Trusted-introducer evidence, separate from bank verification.
5. The bank colleague's accountable CDD decision.
6. The applicant's acceptance of ordinary account terms.
7. Account activation and safe servicing.
8. Applicant access allowed.
9. Supporter access to balances and transactions denied.
10. Illustrative bank and partner operating effort.
11. The reusable capabilities beneath the journey.

The page may mention refugees, care leavers, and abuse survivors only as later applications of the reusable architecture. It must keep its narrative focus on the person experiencing homelessness.

## Language and claim rules

### Use

- Strategic advisory.
- Independent target architecture.
- Working proving ground.
- Interactive demonstration.
- Blueprint.
- Evidence by construction.
- Vendor-neutral working specification.
- Designed for assisted web, telephone, and branch delivery.
- The supporter supplies bounded evidence; the bank makes the CDD decision.

### Do not use without new substantiation

- The only or the first.
- Regulator-accepted or regulator-approved evidence.
- Compliance by construction.
- Signed evidence package.
- Live proposition or live engine.
- Three-minute onboarding.
- Charity-attested identity.
- Anchor Identity as the public proposition name.
- Anchor Edition or any second-class product label.
- Zero PII unless the full journey and every projection have been verified.
- The core becomes irrelevant.

Every new material statement must pass through `docs/claim-register.md`. Demonstration claims must also match the prototype's marketing claim audit.

## Visual direction

This change does not require a visual rebrand. Retain the DTA palette, typography, and established components.

Change the visual hierarchy:

- Put board and customer outcomes before mechanisms.
- Use one human story to create emotional weight.
- Use diagrams only after the proposition is clear.
- Separate Advisory and Talent & Squads with distinct cards and calls to action.
- Keep architecture detail on the Approach and Platform pages.
- Use status badges and evidence labels consistently.

The A Place to Start page should feel dignified and calm. Avoid stock imagery of rough sleeping, staged distress, or visual cues that turn homelessness into spectacle. Use a restrained synthetic-person illustration or journey view only after lived-experience review.

## Measurement

Track the two commercial routes separately:

- Strategic-challenge CTA clicks and completed bookings.
- Talent-and-squad CTA clicks and completed enquiries.
- Homepage-to-proposition visits.
- Visits from A Place to Start into Advisory or Customer Outcomes.
- Proposition engagement and evidence-section interaction.
- Qualified enquiries that name a proposition, architecture decision, or transformation problem.

The design should pass a ten-second comprehension test: a bank executive should identify DTA as strategic advisory, understand that DTA can prove its advice, and see that staffing is available.

## Delivery sequence

### Release 1: Corporate hierarchy

- Update homepage positioning and section order.
- Reorganise navigation around Advisory, Propositions, and Talent & Squads.
- Create the Talent & Squads page.
- Update repeated copy and the messaging map.
- Preserve current demonstration claims.

### Release 2: A Place to Start blueprint

- Add the proposition page and proposition card.
- Label the journey Blueprint.
- Add the human story, channel design, trust boundary, and illustrative operating-cost model.
- Update the claim register with approved external sources and public wording.

### Release 3: Demonstrated journey and evidence

- Promote the proposition only after the engine-backed journey and privacy boundary pass.
- Add the redacted evidence experience only after its schema, projection, and access controls pass.
- Update status badges and the claim register from tested evidence.

## Acceptance criteria

The design is complete when:

- Strategic advisory leads the hero, navigation, and offer order.
- Staffing remains visible from the homepage and primary navigation.
- Visitors can understand and buy either route independently.
- The demonstration supports the advisory claim without making DTA look like a platform vendor.
- A Place to Start leads with one person's outcome and carries an honest status label.
- Every public capability statement agrees with the claim register.
- The site distinguishes demonstrated, illustrative, and blueprint content.
- Mobile navigation and page hierarchy remain accessible.
- Each primary page has one clear action tied to the visitor's intent.

## Out of scope

- A visual rebrand.
- Turning the Clean-Room Demonstration into a licensed software product.
- Publishing internal schemas, policy logic, or private architecture codenames.
- Claiming regulatory approval or compliance certification.
- Implementing the A Place to Start engine journey or evidence package as part of the corporate copy release.
