# Observatory Model Template

Use this template for every Future Banking Observatory model.

The Observatory publishes living operating models, not reports or white papers. Each model explains why a future operating model emerges from today's system pressures, how the future works, and what evidence leaders need before committing capital.

## Five Observatory diagrams

Each model uses the same analytical language:

1. Systems Evolution: why the market is changing.
2. Strategic Interaction: how participants respond.
3. Future Narrative Journey: what the future looks and feels like.
4. Executable Journey: how people, AI, capabilities, architecture, controls and evidence work together.
5. Decision Gate: whether capital should commit.

## Claim guardrail checklist

- Do not present a consultation, proposal or draft regime as settled law.
- Do not imply regulatory endorsement, certification or approval.
- Do not claim DTA has solved a national infrastructure design question.
- Do not attribute DTA views to TSB, regulators, schemes, vendors or clients.
- Do not publish protected internals: schemas, ontology mechanics, control internals, Authority Graph, Claim Register mechanics or non-public runtime design.
- Label every claim as Demonstrated, Specified, Blueprint, Roadmap or Illustrative where the reader might infer proof.
- Use "demonstrated on synthetic data" for Accountable Bank examples.
- Keep the page vendor-neutral.

## Voice guidelines

- Explain before recommending.
- Show systems before solutions.
- Treat architecture as evidence.
- Use board language: authority, accountability, evidence, confidence, capital, outcomes.
- Avoid report language: white paper, research report, downloadable PDF as the main product.
- Say "published when the model changes, not on a schedule" where subscription language appears.
