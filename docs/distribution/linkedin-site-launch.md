## Post A — the launch (main)

After a lot of quiet building, Digital Transformation Advisory is live.

The premise is simple, and a little uncomfortable: a bank cannot be accountable for what it does not control. As agents start to act for customers, and money starts to move as tokens, that stops being a slogan and becomes an engineering problem.

So we built something to make it concrete. Not a slide deck, a working proving ground: one governed, agent-native platform where an agent prepares and proposes, a human approves before anything material happens, and the evidence is recorded at every step. It runs several banking propositions today on synthetic data. It is non-production by design, it never touches real customers, and because it is built on open standards (ISO 20022, BIAN, recognised control frameworks) what it demonstrates doubles as a working specification a bank can take to any vendor.

What that lets us do that the market cannot: test whether a proposition delivers good customer outcomes *before* a bank builds it, including the new questions agents create that no focus group can reach, did the customer understand what the agent did, did they consent, could they step in. With the FCA now supervising for outcomes rather than implementation, that is the question of the moment.

If you are wrestling with agentic banking, Consumer Duty evidence, or how to move without re-platforming, take a look:
👉 [https://digital-transformation-advisory.com]

The architecture thinking is written up in full on the site. Honest about what is demonstrated and what is blueprint, throughout.
