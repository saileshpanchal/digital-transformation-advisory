Financial Intelligence

The question: are you funding the right transformation, at the right time, for the right reasons?

Every transformation competes for scarce capital. AI, cloud, tokenisation and regulatory change all demand investment, yet the one-pager that wins approval assumes a stable operating model that no longer exists. Financial Intelligence is the CFO's decision system for transformation: the evidence to commit capital with confidence, before it is spent, not a forecast explained after.

Capital allocation, answered in four decisions

This is not accounting. It is the sequence of decisions that funds a transformation, each backed by evidence before the money moves.

1 · Should we invest?→ 2 · What is the real cost?→ 3 · Can we afford the transition?→ 4 · Did we create value?

Decision 1

Should we invest?

The first question is not "how much" — it is whether this change deserves scarce capital at all, and what kind of bet it is. It cannot be answered on activity: a bank buys a few thousand AI-assistant licences, and a year on no one can name what improved. Value appears only when the operating model changes, so decide what you will change, and how you will know it moved, before you fund it.

Illustrative. There is no single curve. A new build invests then ramps; a like-for-like replacement runs old and new in parallel for cost-out; an agentic replacement changes the service itself, so the value is both cost-out and new outcomes. Classify the initiative, then fund it for what it is.

Decision 2

What is the real cost?

The one-pager hides it. AI does not add a line to the old model; it adds a new operating-cost stack that shifts spend from fixed technology toward ongoing consumption, governance and oversight.

Traditional

People · Infrastructure · Software. Largely fixed, bought once, depreciated over years.

AI era

AI build · learn · run · support · compute · token budgets · model governance · human oversight. Consumption that scales with usage, plus governance and operational cost that never stops.

Illustrative. The old shape equated value with engineers over a thin support and governance layer. AI-amplified delivery breaks it: AI build, learn and run becomes a major line, governance is a material layer, operational management is its own layer, and infrastructure moves to consumption.

Decision 3

Can we afford the transition?

On a replacement, the money is spent before it is saved. The question is how deep the trough goes, and how deep the combined trough goes across overlapping initiatives.

Old cost→ Dual running→ Peak investment→ Migration→ Decommission→ New cost base

Illustrative. Fund to a known envelope — squad units, compute, token budget. A cap is itself a control: trade-offs are forced and open-ended spend ends. Model the peak cumulative funding across overlapping curves, and the option value of moving early, not a single trough.

Decision 4

Did we create value?

Approval is not the end of the financial decision; it is the first of many. Transformation is a series of proven decisions, governed by checkpoints, not a single sign-off.

Investment→ Funding checkpoint→ Transition checkpoint→ Benefits checkpoint→ Run-rate checkpoint

At each gate the value is tracked projected-against-actual, and the budget — compute and tokens included — can still be redirected. This is the basics of good financial management for the AI era: control that continues through delivery, not a one-off approval.

The CFO in 2030

Finance is changing as fast as the technology it funds.

Traditional finance managed

Capex · Opex · ROI. A stable base, an annual cycle, a forecast.

The AI era adds

Compute budgets · token consumption · agent operating costs · model governance · dual-running economics · regulatory optionality · ecosystem participation. Live budgets, watched continuously.

Financial Intelligence is built for the second list.

Interlocked with the other lenses

Financial Intelligence is the integration layer: each Intelligence contributes to the decision. Follow a lens to go deeper.

Value

Measures the economic benefit: deposits, margin, cost-to-serve, and deposit defence as tokenised money reshapes the balance sheet.

Capability

Determines delivery capacity: squad and supporting-staff cost, the AI-amplified role mix, and the reskilling to get there.

Platform

Shapes technology cost: infrastructure run-cost, dual-running with the core, and multi-ledger coexistence.

Cognitive

Sizes AI build, inference and refresh, evaluation, and the cost of AI governance.

Control

Determines governance overhead: model risk, data and risk governance, and agentic spend controls.

Experience

Keeps consumer outcomes positive, and surfaces value that leaks when a journey is misread.

Trust

The harm avoided, and the value lost when customers never trust the journey.

C3

Governed human-machine action at scale, cutting the hidden supervision and approval-latency costs that quietly destroy AI returns.

Evidence before commitment

Transformation doesn't fail for lack of ambition. It fails when capital is committed before there is enough evidence. Financial Intelligence changes that.
