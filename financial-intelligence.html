Financial Intelligence

The question: can your CFO cost this before you commit, and measure it after?

Most transformation business cases are a one-pager dressed for sign-off. The real costs, and whether the value can even be measured, surface too late: after the money is committed and the CFO was never genuinely bought in. Financial Intelligence puts the full P&L on the table before a proposition starts, and keeps financial control on it after.

You can't manage what you can't measure

A bank buys a few thousand AI-assistant licences. A year on, no one can name what improved. HR says people feel more productive; nobody can state the number. Spreading licences widely created activity, not value.

AI value is organisational before it is technical. A productivity gain that never changes roles, controls or decision rights stays trapped as local convenience and never reaches the P&L. So the discipline starts before the spend: decide what the proposition will change, what you will measure, and how you will know it moved. A licence count is an input, not an outcome.

The financial shape depends on what you're actually doing

There is no single transformation curve. Cost the initiative for what it is: a new build, a like-for-like replacement, or an agentic reinvention. Dual-running belongs to the replacement cases only.

Illustrative. A new build invests then ramps; a like-for-like replacement runs old and new in parallel for cost-out; an agentic replacement changes the service itself, so the value is both cost-out and new outcomes. Run several at once and the curves overlap: the number that matters is peak cumulative funding, not a single trough.

The costs a one-pager hides

Before mobilisation, cost the whole picture, not the licence line.

Build, learn and run

AI is built, trained, evaluated and red-teamed, then run at scale, then refreshed. It is an ongoing cost line, not a one-off build.

Adoption and operationalisation

Integration, setup, and the real work of taking a pilot into production to the same control standard as everything else.

Skills and org redesign

Reskilling, new roles, and the operating model around them. The technology is the cheap part; the organisation is where the cost and the value sit.

Transformation and change

The cost of moving the organisation, not just the platform: dual-running, decommissioning and contract exit on a replacement.

Consumption, not capacity

Cloud and compute units and token budgets that scale with usage. Fund engineering to a fixed capacity, not an open cheque.

Governance

Model risk, evaluation, audit and explainability. With agentic AI this is a material line, not a rounding error.

Illustrative. The old shape equated value with engineers over a thin support and governance layer. AI-amplified delivery breaks it: AI build, learn and run becomes a major line, governance is a material layer, operational management is its own layer, and infrastructure moves to consumption.

On a replacement, the transition is where it's won or lost

Investment and dual-running come first; payback comes later. Knowing where the trough sits, and how deep the combined trough goes across overlapping initiatives, is the point.

Illustrative. The investment-before-return curve for a replacement: dual-running, decommissioning and contract exit. Run several initiatives together and the curves overlap; model the peak cumulative funding and the option value of moving early, not one curve.

Fund to a capacity, then govern it

A known envelope, squad units, compute and token budget, is itself a control. A team that knows its limit works to it, trade-offs get forced, and open-ended spend ends.

Change is funded from the part of the cost base you can actually move, not the whole base. Set the capacity, then keep financial control through delivery: value tracked projected-against-actual, compute and token budgets governed, and stage-gates on the curve where the money can still be redirected. This is the basics of good financial management for the AI era, when engineering, cloud compute and token usage are all live budgets: financial checkpoints, not a one-off business case.
