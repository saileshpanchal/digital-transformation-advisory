Financial Intelligence

The question: do you understand the P&L of this change early enough to commit to it?

Transformations fail when the financial impact lands too late. By the time the numbers are clear the money is committed, the business case has eroded, and the CFO was never really bought in. Financial Intelligence puts the P&L of change on the table at the start, not as an audit at the end.

The cost base, as it really is

Most of the spend is already committed. You can only fund change from the part you can actually move.

The as-is position spans people, process, technology, infrastructure and property, and the suppliers and committed contracts behind them. The distinction that decides what is possible is addressable funding versus committed funding: multi-year licences, outsourced contracts and run-the-bank obligations are locked, and change can only be funded from the addressable slice. A transformation costed against the whole base, rather than the part you can move, is a business case that breaks on contact with the finance function.

The cost shape has changed

The old labour-heavy model no longer holds.

When delivery was people, the doers were the cost: a two-pizza squad was roughly 80% of it, with supporting staff the rest. AI-amplified engineering changes the shape. AI is now a cost line in its own right, both to build and to run. Around it sit setup costs, cloud, DevOps pipelines and data environments, and the governance the model now demands: AI governance, data governance and risk governance. A credible model carries all of them, not just headcount.

What goes in the model

As-is, target and transition, across cost and value.

As-is cost base

People, process, technology, infrastructure and property: what the bank spends to run today.

Suppliers and committed contracts

Third parties, licences and outsourced deals, with their terms and break points.

Addressable vs committed funding

What is actually changeable versus locked in, because change is funded only from the addressable part.

Squad and supporting staff

The AI-amplified squad and the roles around it, on fixed-capacity funding.

AI build and run

Model development and ongoing inference, a real and growing line, not a rounding error.

Setup

Cloud, DevOps pipelines and data environments stood up before value flows.

Governance and control

AI, data and risk governance, security and the cost of control.

Transition and dual-running

Running the old and new worlds together until the new one is proven.

Decommissioning and exit

Retiring legacy and exiting contracts, including break fees.

The value side

Revenue, cost-to-serve reduction, loss and fraud reduction, capital efficiency and retention.

Enough to get the CFO bought in and to scope and time the change.
