Our approach

From strategy to squads.

Most bank and FinTech transformations start with technology. We start with architecture, strategy and the propositions that create value — then build the teams that deliver them, and reshape the business around them.

Where it starts

Architecture and strategy come first

Before a line of code, we work out what proposition creates value, how it makes money, and how the bank must be shaped to deliver it. That decision spans five lenses at once.

Business architecture Domain architecture Technology architecture Enterprise architecture Strategy & proposition design

Proposition, operating model and architecture are one decision, not three sequential ones. Get the order wrong and architecture choices arrive too late to matter.

How delivery is organised

Squads, Domain Building Blocks and value streams

A composable bank is built by small, senior, cross-functional squads — each owning the banking capabilities it delivers, grouped into value streams aligned to propositions.

Domain Building Blocks

A DBB packages a meaningful banking capability — journey, policy, controls, evidence and execution — that can be reused across propositions instead of copied.

Squads own the DBBs

Each squad owns one or more Domain Building Blocks end-to-end. Small enough to move fast, senior enough to be accountable for what they ship.

Value streams (SAFe)

Squads group into value streams aligned to a proposition and its P&L. The SAFe model gives you coordination across squads without re-centralising delivery.

The part FinTechs underestimate

The whole bank reorganises around the squads

The squad model isn't just an engineering chart. The framework of the entire business comes to revolve around the value streams.

Risk, finance, HR, regulatory affairs, compliance, operations — each has to align to how value is now delivered. That is the hard, unglamorous work that decides whether a transformation actually lands.

FinTechs often underestimate the sheer size of the business organisation required to run a real bank — multi-currency, multi-country, multi-regulator. Even an ambitious "zero-ops" model still needs a substantial, accountable business wrapped around it. Automation reduces effort; it does not remove the obligation.

We help you see that whole picture early, so the operating model, the controls and the cost base are designed on purpose rather than discovered in production.
