Our approach

Build the organisation of the future — on purpose.

The bank of the future has to be constructed, not assembled. We bring a strategy-led approach to that construction: a whole-organisation transformation, set out as a high-level vision and a two-year roadmap, that changes people, process and technology together.

The reason it matters now: agents are crossing from advice to action. A regulated bank can only allow that if it is accountable for every action an agent takes and sovereign over the agents, data and models it runs on — because you cannot be accountable for what you do not control. We call the result Owned Autonomy: agentic capability a bank owns rather than rents. That is the destination this approach constructs.

Where it starts

Architecture and strategy come first

Before a line of code, we work out what proposition creates value, how it makes money, and how the organisation must be shaped to deliver it. That decision spans five lenses at once.

Business architecture Domain architecture Technology architecture Enterprise architecture Strategy & proposition design

Proposition, operating model and architecture are one decision, not three sequential ones. From it we set a high-level vision and a roadmap — pitched roughly two years out — that moves people, process and technology in step. Get the order wrong and architecture choices arrive too late to matter.

Built on proven foundations

An opinionated model, on industry-proven frameworks

We don't reinvent what works. We assemble proven industry frameworks into an opinionated model, then customise it to your organisation.

BIAN SAFe TOGAF — ABBs SFIA — people layer DBBs — our term

BIAN, SAFe, TOGAF and SFIA are established standards. SFIA — the Skills Framework for the Information Age — grounds the people layer: every role is a SFIA skills profile, so capability is explicit and portable. Domain Building Blocks (DBBs) are our own term — coined to sit alongside TOGAF's Architecture Building Blocks: where an ABB is a reusable technical capability, a DBB is a reusable banking capability. It's our opinionated view, tailored to you.

How delivery is organised

Squads, Domain Building Blocks and value streams

A composable organisation is built by small, senior, cross-functional squads — each owning the capabilities it delivers, grouped into value streams aligned to propositions.

Domain Building Blocks

A DBB packages a meaningful banking capability — journey, policy, controls, evidence and execution — that is reused across propositions instead of copied. (Our term, alongside TOGAF's ABBs, and the Capability plane of our C3 architecture.) Read the paper →

Squads own the DBBs

Each squad owns one or more Domain Building Blocks end-to-end. Small enough to move fast, senior enough to be accountable for what they ship.

Value streams (SAFe)

Squads group into value streams aligned to a proposition and its P&L. The SAFe model gives you coordination across squads without re-centralising delivery.

The shape of a squad

Deliberately small. One squad, five people, accountable for its Domain Building Blocks.

1Product Owner
1Forward Platform Engineer
2Engineers
1Data Engineer

Every role is defined against SFIA, the global skills standard — so capability is explicit and assessable, not personality-dependent. The Product Owner owns product management, requirements and stakeholder relationships and is the single engagement point to the business. The Forward Platform Engineer (FPE) owns solution architecture, systems integration and consultancy — a forward-deployed role that integrates into your estate, not an in-house tech lead. Engineers own programming, testing and deployment; the Data Engineer owns data, evidence and measurement. (Skill names shown; the full SFIA profiles with levels of responsibility are part of our IP. Built by a SFIA 10 contributor.)

It's a default, not a mandate — AI is blurring role boundaries fast, and SFIA lets us name which skill AI has absorbed rather than argue about job titles. The squad lands as a forward-deployed unit — humans at the edges, agents in the middle, under human direction — bringing its own paved-road controls and integrating outward through the FPE. It runs on fixed-capacity funding: cost is the constant, scope flexes, and the squad owns its cloud and AI run-rate — the brake a CRO asks for.

What's really important

The whole organisation reorganises around the squads

Banks are cutting costs everywhere. The model's value is showing what to protect and invest in — because the squad framework only works when the whole business moves with it.

Our job isn't to add to the cut list. It's to show what's genuinely important — the capabilities, controls and architecture decisions worth protecting — whether you run an established bank, are standing up a new sub-division, or are a founder starting from scratch.

Risk, finance, HR, regulatory affairs, compliance and operations each have to align to how value is now delivered and evidenced. For an established organisation the risk is fragmentation — these functions siloed and duplicated per product. For a new sub-division or a startup it's the opposite: under-building the real bank, then discovering its weight in production.

And a real bank is heavy: multi-currency, multi-country, multi-regulator. Even an ambitious "zero-ops" model still needs a substantial, accountable business wrapped around it. Automation reduces effort; it does not remove the obligation.

Two starting points, one destination

Getting there from where you are

Most of our work is whole-organisation transformation for established banks and FinTechs. The same model also gives teams building something new — a new sub-division, or a startup — a way to start right.

If you already run a bank or FinTech

The hard part is sequencing change while keeping the lights on. We start with architecture and strategy, pick a beachhead proposition, and stand up the first value stream and squads around its Domain Building Blocks. Shadow integration lets you prove value alongside the existing estate without re-platforming — then you expand block by block, migrate the business functions in step, and decommission the old path only once the new one is proven.

If you're building something new — a sub-division or a startup

The hard part is building enough bank without a cost base you can't carry. We start from the proposition, the P&L and the licence perimeter, stand up the first squad around the smallest set of DBBs that makes it real, and build the minimum credible bank underneath — the accountable functions a regulator and a customer actually require, designed on purpose — then compose the next proposition from the same building blocks.

Our proving method

The Glass Tube

Rather than a big-bang programme, we run a single domain end-to-end as a transparent vertical slice — so you can watch, and measure, the real levels of organisational change it demands (and the true cost and revenue delta) before you scale.

The glass tube de-risks the economics: it proves reuse, controls and value on one beachhead so the rest of the estate is priced from evidence, not optimism. It's where the offer lives — the six-week Proposition Blueprint scopes the tube; the optional 90-day Proof runs it.
