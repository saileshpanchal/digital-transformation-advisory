The idea behind a transformation operating model for the AI era

Transformation Intelligence

Organisations know how to buy technology. They do not know how to transform themselves. The estate keeps getting more complex, and the limit is no longer what you can buy. It is how quickly the organisation can see, decide and move. Transformation Intelligence is how DTA measures that, and why the Accountable Bank works.

Transformation Intelligence is the discipline of making, governing and continuously improving organisational decisions. Organisational cognition emerges from the quality, connectedness and evidence of those decisions. The eight intelligences are eight ways of observing the same decisions.

Why transformation has become harder

Two decades ago, organisations became too complex to run technology without architecture, so architecture became inevitable. The same thing has now happened one layer up. The estate is too complex, moving too fast, under too much AI pressure, to transform on instinct. The technology is not the hard part: everyone buys the same cloud, the same models, the same rails. Transformation is won or lost one layer down.

Everyone invests in the top layer. It is funded, visible and well understood. The bottom layer is where transformation actually succeeds or fails, and it is almost entirely unmeasured.

Technology Transformation

Modernise platforms, data and delivery.

AI Acceleration

Increase decision speed, automation and agentic execution.

Organisational Cognition

Ensure the organisation can understand, govern, evidence and adapt what it is doing.

Most organisations mastered enough of the first to survive and are experimenting with the second. The third is where transformation is now won or lost. DTA helps organisations operate in that third phase.

Seven failures no one measures

Down in that bottom layer, every organisation is quietly losing its ability to see itself clearly. No consulting firm, and no tool you own, measures any of it.

Knowledge decay

The reasoning behind decisions leaves when the people do.

Decision decay

Why a choice was made is lost, so it is relitigated a year later.

Capability decay

Skills erode faster than they are rebuilt, and no one can see it.

Architectural drift

The real architecture diverges from the one on the slide.

Governance drift

Controls describe last year, not this quarter.

AI slop

Ungoverned model output quietly pollutes the record of truth.

Reporting distortion

The board sees a version of reality that is no longer true.

The constraint has a name

That bottom layer, the organisation's ability to see, decide and move as fast as the technology changes, is organisational cognition. It is what actually decides the outcome now, and almost no one measures it. Traditional consulting helps you decide what to build. Transformation Intelligence helps you decide whether it should be built at all, what evidence is required before committing, and how to prove it before delivery begins. DTA turns it into a transformation operating model for the AI era: measure the cognition, prove the path, then commit the capital.

Eight dimensions, observed at once

Not eight because we like the number. Eight because a decision has to be read through eight dimensions at the same time. They are eight ways of observing the same decisions, so a change in one is visible in all. Every worked example is built this way in the Workbench.

Decision

Does board strategy survive into delivery? Decision governs from the top.

Architecture

Can the change actually be walked? Architecture is the substrate the rest run on.

Value

Can we see the return early? Value is the economics running through every one.

Accountability

Can we defend it on demand? Accountability is the evidence spine that must hold across all.
