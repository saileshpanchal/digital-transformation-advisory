From governance artefacts to evidence by construction

Continuous assurance instead of periodic review. Delegated authority that is traceable to the action it authorised. Decisions that are audit-ready because the evidence is captured as the work runs, not reconstructed after a complaint. Consent and vulnerability handling are absorbed here: Consumer Duty outcomes become accountability evidence, not a separate exercise.

The result reads like a second ledger. The accounting ledger records value. The trust ledger records authority, evidence, limits and accountability: who was authorised to decide or act, by whom, within what limits, with what evidence, and what happened when the decision was executed. It is not another system of record; it is the board-facing view of the connected evidence this door builds, and it is what a personally liable senior manager stands on when asked to defend reasonable steps.
