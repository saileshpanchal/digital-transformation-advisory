Three themes run through every engagement

They describe how transformation actually happens: keep what matters, run the old and new worlds together, and let people and machines work as one.

Preservation

Retain knowledge, evidence, capability, and institutional memory through change.

Evolution

Operate old and new worlds together and migrate safely, rather than betting the institution on a single cutover.

Augmentation

Enable humans and machines to think and act together, with authority and evidence that hold under scrutiny.

Seven Intelligences

We read a bank through seven lenses. They overlap rather than file the work into boxes, and most propositions draw on several at once. See how the lenses work together.

Experience

The outcomes people need and the journeys they actually live, including those most easily excluded.

Capability

How human and organisational capability is built and evolved, across skills, knowledge, and judgment.

Cognitive

How humans and machines reason and act together, with clear authority, explanation, and intervention.

Value

How value is represented, exchanged, governed, settled, and measured.

Trust

How justified trust is earned through inclusion, consent, privacy, evidence, and resilience.

Platform

How systems evolve and compose, with interoperability, coexistence, governed data, and runtime architecture.

Control

How accountable leaders set authority, observe outcomes, intervene, and evidence control continuously.

Experience Journeys

A journey is the end-to-end experience a person or institution actually lives, distinct from the commercial proposition that contains it. We use journeys to test propositions, authority boundaries, data products, and consumer outcomes before a bank commits to build.

A Place to Start Blueprint

Help one person without a fixed address, conventional documents, or a smartphone gain an ordinary basic bank account, while the bank keeps accountability and the applicant keeps control.

Synthetic Bank Migration Twin Blueprint

Model a plausible legacy bank with synthetic data, prove the target architecture and migration touchpoints, and produce a vendor-neutral specification before a bank buys or migrates a core.

Proof, not slides

{{ site.data.copy.canonical.clean_room }}

Because it is expressed in open standards, ISO 20022 messaging, BIAN domain boundaries, and recognised control frameworks, the demonstration doubles as a working specification: portable, reusable, and ready for whatever vendor a bank chooses to build with.

The boundary is explicit. The data is synthetic, the consumers are test consumers and never real customers, the controls are designed to production grade, and nothing here runs a production banking service. Planned behaviour is labelled Blueprint or Specified, never Demonstrated.

Leadership

Sailesh Panchal, Founder & CEO

Sailesh is a technology, payments and transformation leader in UK financial services and FinTech. As CTO and Payments & Economic Crime Director at TSB Bank, he led a bank-wide transformation that brought technology and business leadership closer together. It simplified the cost base, accelerated delivery, reduced fraud, supported growth in payments, unified architecture and established the bank's AI Centre of Excellence.

He has built regulated banks from the ground up and shaped national infrastructure. As Group CTO at Orwell Group he conceived the ipagoo platform, the first cloud-first regulated EMI certified by the FCA and ACPR on AWS and Azure, live on the UK payment schemes in months and winner of the 2018 FIA Award. As Chief Architect for the Payment Systems Regulator he led the New Payments Architecture, and he has contributed to Bank of England, ISO 20022 and Open Banking committees.

Earlier roles include Director of Engineering at Metro Bank (Agile/SAFe at scale), Head of Regulatory Architecture at Lloyds Banking Group (a £250m+ portfolio spanning FSCS, CASS, payM and PSD2/Open Banking), CTO at Travelex and Chief Architect at JPMorgan. He founded Digital Transformation Advisory in 2019 and is writing a book on digital transformation in UK banking.
