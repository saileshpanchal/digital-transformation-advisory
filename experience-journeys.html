Experience Journeys

A journey is the end-to-end experience a person or institution actually lives. We use journeys to test propositions, authority, governed data, and consumer outcomes before a bank commits to build, because a good journey is never just an experience problem: it pulls several Intelligences together at once.

A journey is not the same as a proposition

A journey is the experience a person or institution lives, end to end. A proposition is the commercial or product concept a bank sells. One proposition can contain several journeys, and a single journey usually draws on several Intelligences at once. We lead with the journey because that is where outcomes are won or lost, and because it exposes the assumptions a product description hides.

Journeys connect the lenses

A journey is where several Intelligences meet at once. The person lives one path; underneath sit governed knowledge, human-and-agent reasoning, controls, and economics.
