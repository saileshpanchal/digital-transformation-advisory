Cognitive Intelligence

The question: where should humans and AI think together?

AI changes who thinks. Cognitive Intelligence determines how people and machines think together. The question is no longer whether AI will take part in decisions; it is where reasoning should happen, what stays human, what can be delegated to an agent, and how both work together safely. The goal is not to replace human judgment, but to place it where it creates the greatest value.

Reasoning allocation

The question isn't what AI can automate. It's where reasoning should happen, and how much of the decision a person keeps.

Human only→ Human + AI support→ AI, human approves→ AI, human oversees→ Autonomous

Different decisions sit at different points on this scale. Copilots, agents and multi-agent systems each have a place, chosen deliberately for the decision, not applied by default. Here is one allocation: the agent does the preparation, a human authorises anything material, and the evidence is a by-product.

Illustrative. Reasoning is allocated deliberately; authority for material action stays human, and the decision is explained and evidenced.

The cognitive architecture

Above the tooling. Five stages, each allocated between people and machines, and it starts with memory, not a prompt.

Knowledge→ Memory→ Context→ Reasoning→ Decision→ Action

Understand

Knowledge, context and memory: the institutional understanding an answer rests on.

Reason

Retrieval, planning and decision support: working from authoritative context, not guesswork.

Collaborate

People, copilots and agents, each doing the part of the thinking they are best placed to do.

Learn

Feedback, evaluation and continuous improvement, so the system gets better and stays inspectable.

An organisation's reasoning starts with institutional memory, not a prompt. Governed knowledge decides what an agent is allowed to know before Control decides whether it may act: the ontology is sovereign, similarity is only advisory. That is also where this lens meets Capability — the judgment a person brings to the decision.

Cognitive and Control: two halves of one operating model

One defines how decisions are made. The other defines how they remain accountable. Together they are the operating model for reasoning in a regulated institution.

Cognitive Intelligence

How decisions are made. Where humans and AI reason together: allocation, knowledge, memory, planning and collaboration.

Control Intelligence

How decisions remain accountable. Authority, policy, evidence, intervention and assurance, carried on every action.

Judgment, allocated across three lenses. Capability asks do we have the judgment? Cognitive asks where should that judgment execute? Control asks how do we govern it?
