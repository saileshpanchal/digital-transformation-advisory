Cognitive Intelligence

The question: where does reasoning sit between human and machine, and who may act?

Cognitive Intelligence is how humans and machines reason and act together: where reasoning sits, what an agent may do, and how a decision is explained and intervened in. It is reasoning in operation, distinct from the latent capability a team holds.

Outcomes it governs

Allocation of reasoning

What the agent prepares and proposes, and what stays a human judgement.

Authority to act

A human authorises any material action; authority is explicit, per action and per context.

Explainability

Why a decision was made, recorded as evidence rather than reconstructed later.

Intervention and learning

How a person steps in, overrides, and how the system improves from it.

Where we show it

Our reference architecture for governed human-machine action is Capability, Context and Consent (C3). In the demonstration an agent prepares and proposes, a human approves before anything material happens, and the evidence is recorded at each step.

Related Intelligences
